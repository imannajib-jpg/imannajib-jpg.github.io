About

I started building at 15. I founded FIRST Robotics Team 5130 at a school with no computer science or STEM teachers, taught myself to program from public forums and documentation, and made the case to the local school board for real investment in STEM, securing $10,000 in funding for the program.

Since then I have led the team to the national championship and still mentor it today. I care most about systems I can own end to end, where the mechanical, electrical, and software pieces all have to work together under real conditions, not just on a bench.

I study Computer Science and Political Science at Williams College.
