About

I started building at 15. I founded FIRST Robotics Team 5130 at a school with no computer science or STEM teachers, taught myself to program from public forums and documentation, and made the case to the local school board for real investment in STEM, securing $10,000 in funding for the program.

// overview

Umizoomi is my full competition robot, named for its omnidirectional swerve drivetrain. It is built on an aluminum frame with custom polycarbonate panels, a 14-device CAN bus network, a Pigeon 2 IMU, a high-ratio climber, and a custom shooter gearbox I designed in Fusion 360. Programmed in Java on WPILib.

// drivetrain
  • Omnidirectional swerve drive on an aluminum frame, which is where the name comes from.
  • Spark MAX motor controllers daisy-chained on a 14-device CAN bus network.
  • CTRE Pigeon 2 IMU for field-relative orientation and balancing.
// mechanisms
  • Custom climber subsystem geared at a higher reduction ratio to supply the torque needed to lift the robot.
  • Custom shooter gearbox designed in Fusion 360.
// electrical & power
  • 12V battery feeding a Power Distribution Panel through correctly gauged wiring.
  • All motor controllers and sensors networked over CAN bus.
// fabrication
  • Fully modeled in Fusion 360 before building.
  • Structural panels started in acrylic and were upgraded to polycarbonate to eliminate stress fractures under competition loads.

// overview

After building Umizoomi's hardware, I implemented the full vision processing and autonomous routing stack on top of it. This involved integrating a Limelight smart camera as a dedicated vision coprocessor, calibrating it for AprilTag detection, and wiring the pose output into the control loop so the robot can target and align autonomously. PathPlanner handles the autonomous field routines.

// limelight vision
  • Limelight 3 mounted on the robot as a standalone coprocessor running its own vision pipeline.
  • AprilTag calibration performed using the Limelight documentation to achieve accurate field-relative pose estimation.
  • Pose data fed into the robot's control loop for closed-loop targeting and autonomous alignment.
  • USB webcam streamed separately to the driver station for operator view alongside the Limelight feed.
// pathplanner autonomy
  • PathPlanner used to author and deploy full field autonomous routines, including multi-step paths and timed sequences.
  • Autonomous paths tested and tuned iteratively against physical robot performance.
